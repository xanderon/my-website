We had lying around an IKEA GRUNDVATTNET that we used from time to time, but because of its size, once we were done with it, we usually just left it on the kitchen counter.

And in our old kitchen, counter space is a luxury 😄

So we moved the IKEA GRUNDVATTNET to the pantry.
Much better for the counter, not that great when you actually need to grab it.

So here we go again :)

We looked around the kitchen to find a better spot for it. The wall next to the sink was kind of empty, so we placed it there just to get a feel for it.

And it looked good. Also, the position felt pretty handy.

Next up: the design.

I kept this one super simple: basically an extended arch that holds the GRUNDVATTNET in place at both ends.

I went to Corel and drew the section, then moved to Tinkercad and made a simple extrude.

And that was kind of it.

I printed a small 2 cm test piece first, just to see if it fits.

It did.

After that, I extended the model a bit more, so it would hold better in place.

Looking at the design in Tinkercad, it felt a bit too basic, so I looked around the Tinkercad shapes and found some squiggly lines.

I embossed them into the model, just about 1 mm deep.

And it actually looked pretty good.

<figure class="blog-figure is-narrow">
  <img src="/blog-assets/ikea-grundvattnet/tinkercad%20model.jpeg" alt="IKEA GRUNDVATTNET holder model in Tinkercad with embossed lines" />
  <figcaption>The model in Tinkercad, with the embossed lines.</figcaption>
</figure>

Printed two holders, one for each end of the IKEA GRUNDVATTNET.

Did a quick dry placement with the GRUNDVATTNET and the two holders, and everything seemed fine.

Then I added white double-sided tape, kept the GRUNDVATTNET in place to make sure the distance between the holders was correct, pressed them onto the wall, and that was that :)

PS: I also tried to print the squiggly / wavy lines separately, just to see if I could fit them into the embossed area.

But because I printed the holders vertically, the embossed area came out with slightly sloped walls instead of straight ones, so the inserts didn't really fit.

Not a big deal, just something extra I wanted to experiment with :)
