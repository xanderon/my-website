Given the limited kitchen space in an old 1986 apartment building, I always need to find hidden but still accessible places to put stuff.

About 1 year ago we had a circular kitchen scale. Our 3D printer was quite new in our home back then and I wanted to see if I could model a holder for it. So I did a few Fusion 360 tutorials and after some trial and error I managed to make something pretty nice.

But... yeah... in time it bent.

It was quite close to the stove and I printed it in PLA. Heat + time = not great 😄
And because it bent, the scale started to slip out of the holder. At some point it even started acting weird, even with a fresh battery. Probably from a few small falls... soooo yeah.

---

Luckily I found a kitchen scale at Lidl for like 10 Lei. So... around 2 euro / dollars.
Nice.

But... same problem again.

Where do I put it so it's not in the way, but also not annoying to grab every time?

I checked MakerWorld. There are some models there, but none really fit how I wanted it. So... c'est la vie -> Tinkercad time.

---

So I measured the scale, traced the curve on paper like a caveman, then moved that into CorelDRAW to make it a vector, and from there into Tinkercad.

but future me will handle that 😄

---

I didn't print the whole thing from the start.

I did a few small test prints first, like ~2 cm slices, just to see if it fits. Way faster, way less wasted filament.

After I got that somewhat right:

- added holes for screws
- tested with random screws I had around

Here I had a small issue. I needed the screws to be short, but also flush.
If not, the scale would hit / scratch every time I take it out or put it back. And that would get annoying fast.

---

I also added a stopper at the back, just to be safe, so it doesn't go too far in.

And I was also thinking... glass + plastic -> this thing might just slowly slide out.
So I added two small round bumps at the front so the scale stays slightly angled up.

Also left a very very slim area where the screw actually holds.
Was a bit "hmm this might snap", but I tested it and it seems fine.

---

So... of course... when everything looked good -> print.

2 pieces.

And of course... both the same side.

I forgot to mirror one.

Nice.

Printed again :))

---

Did a dry fit first -> all good.

Then I added some double sided tape and, with the scale in place, I positioned it where I wanted it, same general area as before. This time I printed it in Sunlu PETG. Fingers crossed this one doesn't do the same bending thing.

<figure class="blog-figure is-narrow">
  <img src="/blog-assets/kitchen-scale-holder-v2/holder_installed_closeup.jpeg" alt="Kitchen scale holder installed close-up" />

After that:

- removed the scale
- added screws in all 4 holes
- made sure everything sits flush

Screws went in nicely. No weird bumps, nothing sticking out. I even pulled a bit on the holders and they feel solid enough.

---

In / out with the kitchen scale -> all good.

Looks nice. Feels practical.

First few days it was a bit tighter, as usual, but now it slides in / out nicely.
